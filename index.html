Dr. Erik Kjeang, Director of SFU FCReL of Mechatronic Systems Engineering has an opening for an undergraduate co-op student to work as a Research Assistant on Fuel Cell related projects in collaboration with Ballard Power systems.

The research assistant will become a member of our research team working on fuel cell durability investigations in collaboration with Ballard Power Systems. This work is relevant for zero-emission hydrogen fuel cell vehicles, for which durability and operational reliability are essential. One such research project aims to establish fundamental understanding of membrane degradation processes responsible for decreased fuel cell operation in automotive systems. Another research project aims to identify water transport phenomena within a working fuel cell. The coop student will be involved in experimental analysis and testing as well as in the automation of experimental workflows.

The student will be responsible for some or all of the following:
  • Fabrication of membrane electrode assemblies (MEAs)
  • Assembly of fuel cell stacks and set-up of experiments
  • Execution of performance and durability (AST) fuel cell tests
  • Failure analysis (including identification of root cause & failure modes)
  • Hypothesis testing & Material characterization
  • Collecting and managing test data & Data analysis and image processing
  • Summarizing technical results in formal report(s)
  • Preparing and presenting results at project meetings
Requirements
Key skills include:
  • Ability to work efficiently in a very active laboratory environment.
  • Strong mechanical aptitude to support hands-on work
  • Ability to work independently on tasks/projects (limited supervision)
  • Strong written and verbal communications skills
  • Detail-oriented and methodical in executing plans
  • Ability to critically analyze scientific data
  • Arrive at logical conclusions from analyses of data sets
  • Data analysis using Excel and/or other common software
The following skills and attributes are favourable
  • Understanding of fuel cell operation.
  • Experience working in a laboratory environment.
An individual working towards a bachelor's degree in Mechatronic Systems Engineering, Materials Engineering; Mechanical or Chemical Engineering is preferred. Preference will be given to students who have completed the third year of their degree and are eligible for NSERC funding.
